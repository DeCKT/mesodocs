Meso MMO

A work-in-progress collection of documents created in the development of yet to be named Mesoamerican based MMORPG.

Introduction

Introduction

Runescape-like MMORPG with elements like skill training, social interactions, intriguing combat, equipment collection, fashion collection, and completionist activities.

Skills

There will be 4 categories of skills

  • Combat
  • Collection
  • Production
  • Support

Skills Start at level 1, and max out at level 60. Skills will have various ways to train, with easier methods providing less progress towards levels. Harder, more intensive methods provide quicker ways to level up a skill. At the most efficient methods, a skill should take about 100 hours to level up all the way.

Combat

Other MMOs will utilize 3 forms of combat, being melee, ranged, and magic. Melee will focus on utilizing weapons such as swords. Ranged will focus on utilizing ranged weaponry like bows and arrows. Magic is unique in that instead of using physical weapons to deal damage, damage is done to opponents via magical spells. Many times magic will require some sort of resource, being magical runes, mana, or energy. This game, while not exactly non-fiction, is less based in fantasy, and so the available combat skills for a player to train will be melee and ranged.

The game Runescape, which this game is highly inspired by, provides what is called a combat triangle to help balance the differnt forms of combat. A warrior's armor is strong against the piercing ammunition from an archer, while especially weak against the powerful spells of a mage. Similarly, an archer's lightweight leather armor is easily shredded by the sharp tools of a warrior, while absorbing and counteracting the damage of a mage's spells. A mage's robes are not protective at all, and are easily pierced by an archer's arrows, bolts or knives. However, the advantage that a mage has is the ability to keep their distance from their warrior target.

The balance of this game is inspired by that of Runescape, however, with only 2 forms of combat, in order to be competetive a player must utilize both forms of combat.

Melee

The Melee skill will be primarily utilized in combat against human opponents and larger, less agile animals like bears, boars, buffalo, or llamas. The Melee skill will be the more difficult of the two combat styles to train, as truly gaining experience will require dangerous hand-to-hand encounters.

Ranged

The Ranged skill will be mostly utilized in hunts of wild animals, stealthy encounters, or as a way to weaken or otherwise inhibit a human opponent before engaging in hand-to-hand combat. The Ranged skill will be the safer of the two combat skills to train since it can be done at a distance. However, in combat it will be much less effective since the skill usually involves throwing or shooting something, immediately losing access to that object. While it can be dangerous to anger an opponent from a distance, those with more advanced Ranged skills will be able to dispatch their target before needing to face them head on.

Collection

Mining

While the base idea of Mining being the collection of stones and ores remains consistent with many other instances in popular RPGs, Mining in this game will not focus much on delving into caves and finding rich ore veins using strong pickaxes. As this game is more loosely based on the advances of the Meso-American people, ores and metals will not be very well refined or easily acquired. Metals used will primarily be copper and gold, with more endgame progress giving the player access to small amounts of iron.

One of the main uses of mining will be acquiring stone. Depending on the area the player finds themself in, they will be able to find smaller stones to use for rudimentary tools like knives. Larger amounts of stone can be collected with tools, allowing for advances in things like fireplaces with cooking, or furnaces for smelting alloys.

Trade

The Trade skill will enable players to exchange goods and equipment with other players and with NPCs. Higher levels of trade will enable players to get better deals, to have better estimates on item values, and more easily develop relationships with NPCs from cities, towns and villages other than their own. The Trade skill can be tightly interwoven with just about any other skill, buying and selling animal pelts, refined materials, armor and weapons, and so on.

Inspired by the player-driven economy of the MMO Albion Online, certain resources will be less available in different areas, and so a potential way to gain wealth is to acquire resources, tools, or equipment in an area where those items are plentiful and more accessible and to transport those items and trade them.

Husbandry

Husbandry is the process of raising and caring for animals. Training this skill will begin with either capturing a wild animal through the Hunting skill, or purchasing wild or already domesticated animals through the Trade skill. Animals will then be cared for through the creation of habitats, feeding, training, and breeding.

There will be several tiers of animals which can be managed. Lower tier animals will be easier to deal with, but will provide less benefits to the player. Higher tiers will be more difficult but much more rewarding. Not all animals will serve the same purpose. There will be some animals which only really are used for their products, such as meats, pelts, feathers, eggs, or other animal products. Other animals will serve as beasts of burden or as combat and hunting companions.
